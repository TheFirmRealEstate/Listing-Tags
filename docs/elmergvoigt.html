In Memory of Elmer G. Voigt

You may find this interesting: Elmer Voigt was the original owner for whom this home was built.

Address: 3225 Michigan Blvd.

Elmer G. Voigt, age 76, passed away March 14, 1962 in South Side District Hospital, Mesa, Arizona after a brief illness. Mr. Voigt was born in Milwaukee, Wisconsin on December 17, 1885.

He was a former chairman of the Board and director of Western Publishing Co., Inc. He was a member of the English Evangelical Lutheran Church of the Epiphany, located at 2921 Olive Street.

Survived by:

  • Wife: Nellie Voigt
  • Son and daughter-in-law: Mr. and Mrs. Ralph G. (Doris) Voigt of Racine
  • Four grandchildren
  • Three sisters: Mrs. Ida Mantey (Watertown), Mrs. Charlotte Steffen (Marathon), Mrs. Hertha Liebrieht (Wauwatosa)
  • Two brothers: George Voigt (Madison) and Alvin Voigt (Brookfield)
  • Numerous nieces, nephews, and other relatives

Funeral Details:

Funeral services were held Saturday, March 17, at 10 a.m. at the English Evangelical Lutheran Church of the Epiphany. Rev. Edwin A. Jaster officiated. Interment was in Mound Cemetery. Mr. Voigt lay in state at the church from 9 a.m. until the time of services.

Pallbearers:

  • Ray Jondahl
  • Harold Mason
  • Gilbert E. Brach
  • Harold Hornburg
  • Tom Laffey Sr.
  • Arthur J. Christiansen

Honorary pallbearers included members of the Board of Directors of Western Publishing Co., Inc.

Memorials:

The family requested that expressions of sympathy in memory of Mr. Voigt be made in memorials to:

  • Racine YMCA
  • St. Luke’s Hospital
  • Educational Council of Graphic Arts Industry, Inc. (Washington, D.C.)
  • English Evangelical Lutheran Church of the Epiphany

Arrangements by: Wilson Funeral Home, 1139 Blaine Ave.
