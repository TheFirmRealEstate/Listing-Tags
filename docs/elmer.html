This home was originally built for Elmer G. Voigt, a key figure at Western Printing & Lithographing Company during its golden years in Racine, Wisconsin.
Who was Elmer G. Voigt?

Elmer G. Voigt was a longtime executive at Western Printing & Lithographing Company, a cornerstone of Racine’s business history. He was instrumental in the company's success during the mid-20th century. This home was built for him in the early 1950s, a reflection of both his professional achievement and the confidence of the post-war publishing boom.

About Western Printing & Lithographing

Western Printing & Lithographing Co., later known as Western Publishing, was founded in Racine in the early 20th century. It became best known for producing the Little Golden Books, a series of affordable and beautifully illustrated children's books that shaped generations of young readers. At its height, Western Publishing was a national leader in children's literature, comics, puzzles, and educational materials. Its contributions to American culture—especially mid-century childhood—were significant.

Why This Home Matters

Knowing the original owner and purpose behind a home brings a new depth of appreciation. This house wasn’t just another postwar build—it was crafted for someone with stature in one of Racine’s most influential companies. The design reflects a mid-century blend of practicality and distinction, suited for a respected community figure. If walls could talk, they’d speak of editorial meetings, publishing milestones, and Racine’s vibrant mid-century heyday.
